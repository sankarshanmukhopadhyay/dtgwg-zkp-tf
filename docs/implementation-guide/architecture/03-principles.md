# Architectural principles

## AP-01 — Privacy is contextual

Every privacy claim identifies a context, adversary, and horizon.

## AP-02 — Proofs prove statements, not reality

A proof of an attestation does not prove that the upstream real-world determination
was correct.

## AP-03 — Assurance is governance-backed

Cryptography carries privacy and integrity. Accreditation, policy, audit, and
accountability carry assurance.

## AP-04 — Personhood is not civil identity

Policy-defined personhood remains separate from identification.

## AP-05 — Holder control is not authority

Key control does not establish agent delegation or transaction authority.

## AP-06 — Context determines permitted linkability

Correlatable outputs must be intentional, scoped, purpose-bound, and documented.

## AP-07 — Lifecycle is part of trust

Expiry, suspension, revocation, recovery, policy migration, and algorithm migration
are architectural concerns.

## AP-08 — Interoperability includes semantics

Common encodings are insufficient when verifiers infer different meanings.

## AP-09 — Accessibility is a first-class requirement

Alternative interaction paths must preserve explicit assurance semantics.

## AP-10 — Production readiness is systemic

Production readiness emerges from cryptography, governance, interoperability,
operations, accessibility, and redress working together.
